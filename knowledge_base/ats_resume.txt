=== ATS OPTIMISATION & RESUME WRITING KNOWLEDGE BASE ===

--- TOPIC: WHAT IS ATS (APPLICANT TRACKING SYSTEM) ---
An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software used by recruiters to scan, parse, and rank resumes before a human reads them. Over 95% of Fortune 500 companies use ATS. A resume that fails ATS parsing never reaches a recruiter's desk, regardless of qualifications.

Key ATS Principles:
1. Keyword density: ATS scores resumes based on how many role-specific keywords match the job description.
2. Parseable formatting: ATS cannot reliably read tables, columns, headers/footers, text boxes, images, or unusual fonts.
3. Standard section headers: Use "Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Certifications" — not creative alternatives.
4. File format: Submit PDF (with text layer) or DOCX unless TXT is specifically requested.

--- TOPIC: ATS-OPTIMISED RESUME STRUCTURE ---
Recommended section order for maximum ATS and human readability:
1. Name & Contact Information (name, email, phone, LinkedIn, GitHub)
2. Professional Summary (2-3 lines, keyword-rich)
3. Technical Skills (flat list or categorised)
4. Work Experience (reverse chronological, bullet points with metrics)
5. Projects (technical projects with technologies used)
6. Education (degree, university, GPA if >= 3.5)
7. Certifications (name, issuer, date)
8. Achievements & Awards (optional but valuable)

--- TOPIC: KEYWORD OPTIMISATION STRATEGY ---
Step 1: Copy the full job description into a word cloud tool. Identify the top 15-20 most frequent technical and role-specific terms.
Step 2: Compare against your resume. For each missing keyword, find a genuine experience or skill you can map it to.
Step 3: Mirror exact terminology from the JD. If the JD says "RESTful APIs", use "RESTful APIs" — not "REST APIs" or "web services".
Step 4: Distribute keywords throughout — summary, skills section, AND experience bullets. Not just the skills list.
Step 5: Never keyword-stuff (listing skills you don't have). This is detected at the interview stage.

--- TOPIC: QUANTIFYING ACHIEVEMENTS ---
ATS and recruiters both score resumes higher when achievements are quantified.
Weak: "Improved application performance"
Strong: "Optimised PostgreSQL query execution, reducing average API response time from 850ms to 120ms (86% improvement)"

Frameworks for quantification:
- X% improvement: "Reduced deployment time by 40%"
- Volume metrics: "Processed 50,000 records daily"
- Scale metrics: "Led a team of 5 engineers"
- Time metrics: "Delivered 2 weeks ahead of schedule"
- Cost/value metrics: "Saved $15,000 annually in infrastructure costs"

--- TOPIC: COMMON ATS DISQUALIFIERS ---
1. Resume submitted as a scanned image (no text layer)
2. Applicant address used as a header
3. Contact info placed in a document header/footer (ATS cannot parse)
4. Using tables or multi-column layouts (most ATS parsers fail)
5. Skills listed as a word cloud image rather than text
6. Fancy fonts (use Arial, Calibri, or Georgia)
7. Missing or non-standard section headers
8. File name with spaces: use "FirstName_LastName_Resume.pdf"

--- TOPIC: TAILORING YOUR RESUME ---
A generic resume has a 6% callback rate. A tailored resume has a 37% callback rate.

For each application:
1. Match your Professional Summary to the specific role.
2. Reorder your Technical Skills so the most relevant appear first.
3. Rewrite your top 2-3 experience bullets to align with the JD's priorities.
4. Use the company's name and the exact job title somewhere in the document.

--- TOPIC: CERTIFICATIONS THAT BOOST ATS SCORES ---
High-value certifications that appear in JD keyword lists for tech roles:
- Cloud: AWS Certified (Developer, Architect, Cloud Practitioner), GCP Associate, Azure AZ-900/AZ-104
- DevOps/Containers: CKA (Certified Kubernetes Administrator), Docker DCA
- IBM: IBM AI Engineering Professional Certificate, IBM Cloud Essentials (SkillsBuild)
- Data: Google Data Analytics, IBM Data Science Professional Certificate
- Security: CompTIA Security+, AWS Security Specialty
- Agile: PMI-ACP, Scrum Master (PSM I)
