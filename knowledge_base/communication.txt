=== COMMUNICATION SKILLS FOR TECHNICAL INTERVIEWS ===

--- TOPIC: WHY COMMUNICATION MATTERS IN TECH INTERVIEWS ---
Technical skills get you the interview. Communication skills get you the offer.
Studies show that 75% of rejected candidates at major tech firms are eliminated due to communication issues — not technical gaps. Interviewers evaluate not just WHAT you know, but HOW you explain it.

--- TOPIC: THINK-ALOUD METHODOLOGY ---
The most valued skill in a technical interview is narrating your thought process.
Before writing any code or answering any question:
1. Restate the problem in your own words to confirm understanding.
2. Clarify ambiguities: "Should I handle null inputs?", "Is the array sorted?"
3. Walk through your approach before coding: "I'll use a sliding window because..."
4. Explain trade-offs: "This approach is O(n log n) — there's an O(n²) brute force alternative."
5. After solving, discuss optimisations: "This could be improved by caching intermediate results."

Weak: Silently writing code for 20 minutes.
Strong: "I'm thinking a hash map would give us O(1) lookup here, which avoids the nested loop..."

--- TOPIC: STRUCTURED ANSWER FRAMEWORKS ---

1. STAR Method (Behavioural Questions):
   Situation → Task → Action → Result
   Use for: "Tell me about a time...", "Describe a situation where..."

2. PREP Framework (Opinion/Opinion Questions):
   Point → Reason → Example → Point (restate)
   Use for: "What do you think about microservices?", "Do you prefer SQL or NoSQL?"

3. The Feature-Benefit-Impact Framework (Project descriptions):
   Feature: "I built a FAISS vector search module"
   Benefit: "which reduced query latency from 1.2s to 80ms"
   Impact: "enabling the product to scale to 100,000 daily active users"

--- TOPIC: CLARITY AND BREVITY PRINCIPLES ---
Interviewers assess information density. Aim for:
- Short sentences when explaining complex concepts (average 15-20 words per sentence)
- No filler phrases: "um", "like", "you know", "basically"
- Answer the question asked — don't pad with irrelevant background
- Conclude your answer clearly: "...and that's why I'd choose Redis for this use case."

--- TOPIC: HANDLING UNKNOWN QUESTIONS ---
When you don't know the answer:
DO:
- "I haven't worked with that specific technology, but here's how I'd approach learning it..."
- "I'm not certain of the exact syntax, but the conceptual approach would be..."
- "That's at the edge of my knowledge. My best understanding is X — can you confirm that's the right direction?"

DON'T:
- Lie or confabulate an incorrect answer
- Say "I don't know" and stop there — this reads as disengaged
- Ask the interviewer to tell you the answer

--- TOPIC: PACE, PAUSE, AND PRESENCE ---
Effective interview communication pace:
- Speak at 130-150 words per minute (normal conversation is 160-180 wpm — slow down slightly)
- Pause for 2-3 seconds before answering complex questions (shows thoughtfulness, not weakness)
- Use pauses to collect thoughts rather than filler words
- Maintain consistent energy level throughout — energy drops after 45 minutes in long interviews

--- TOPIC: VIRTUAL INTERVIEW COMMUNICATION ---
For remote/video interviews:
1. Look at the camera, not the screen — this creates simulated eye contact.
2. Use a plain, professional background.
3. Ensure stable internet — have a mobile hotspot as backup.
4. Mute notifications on all devices before the call.
5. Keep your setup lit from the front (not behind you).
6. Speak slightly slower than normal to compensate for audio compression.
7. Confirm the interviewer can hear you clearly at the start.

--- TOPIC: CLOSING THE INTERVIEW ---
End every interview with 2-3 prepared questions:
Strong closing questions:
- "What does success look like for someone in this role in the first 90 days?"
- "What are the biggest technical challenges the team is currently solving?"
- "How does the team approach learning new technologies and upskilling?"
- "What's your favourite part about working on this team?"

Avoid:
- "What's the salary?" (too early unless they bring it up)
- "Will I get the job?" (pressure-creating)
- "Can you tell me what you covered earlier?" (shows you weren't paying attention)
