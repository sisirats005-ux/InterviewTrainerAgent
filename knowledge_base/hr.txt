=== HR INTERVIEW KNOWLEDGE BASE ===

--- TOPIC: CORE HR QUESTIONS ---
- "Tell me about yourself": The response should follow the Present-Past-Future formula. Talk about your current role and accomplishments, then transition to how you got there (past experiences/education), and finally connect your career goals to the opportunity at the company (future).
- "Why do you want to work here?": Show that you have researched the company. Discuss their mission, products, engineering culture, or specific challenges they are facing, and connect them to your skills and interests.
- "What are your strengths and weaknesses?":
  - Strengths: Share 1-2 professional strengths with concrete examples (e.g. problem-solving, rapid learning, teamwork).
  - Weaknesses: Share a real but non-critical weakness, and explain the steps you are actively taking to improve it (e.g. public speaking, delegating tasks).

--- TOPIC: CAREER PLAN & EXPECTATIONS ---
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?": The company wants to see commitment and ambition. Explain how you want to grow technically or managerially within the company, showing alignment with their potential career paths.
- "Why are you leaving your current job?": Keep it positive. Never badmouth past employers or colleagues. Focus on seeking new challenges, learning opportunities, or shifts in career direction.

--- TOPIC: CONFLICT RESOLUTION & WORK ETHIC ---
- "How do you handle pressure or tight deadlines?": Focus on prioritization, project planning, transparent communication with stakeholders, and stress management techniques.
- "How do you handle feedback or criticism?": Show coachability and growth mindset. Explain that you view constructive feedback as a tool for personal and professional growth, and give an example of how you implemented feedback to achieve a better outcome.

--- HR INTERVIEW QUESTIONS & MODEL ANSWERS ---
Q: Why should we hire you?
A: Frame your answer around three pillars: (1) Your technical skills are a direct match for the job requirements. (2) Your experience in similar projects shows you can hit the ground running. (3) Your enthusiasm, work ethic, and cultural alignment mean you will be a positive addition to the team. Structure it as: "Based on what we've discussed, it sounds like you need someone who can... My background in... shows I can do exactly that. Furthermore, I am excited about your company's values because..."

Q: What are your salary expectations?
A: Try to defer giving a specific number until you understand the full scope of the role and benefits, or provide a research-backed range. Example: "I'm open to discussing compensation once we determine if I'm a good fit for this role. Based on my research for similar roles in this area and my qualifications, I would expect a starting salary in the range of $X to $Y. However, I am flexible and open to looking at the entire benefits package."
