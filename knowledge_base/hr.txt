=== HR INTERVIEW KNOWLEDGE BASE ===

--- TOPIC: THE HR INTERVIEW PURPOSE ---
The HR (Human Resources) interview evaluates:
1. Cultural fit — do your values align with the company's?
2. Motivation — why do you want this specific role at this specific company?
3. Stability and career planning — will you stay long enough to justify hiring costs?
4. Professionalism — are you easy to work with, communicate clearly, and handle pressure?

Note: In many companies, the HR interview is the LAST step. Failing here after clearing technical rounds is more common than candidates expect. Treat HR with the same preparation rigour as technical rounds.

--- TOPIC: THE MOST COMMON HR QUESTIONS ---

Q: "Tell me about yourself."
Framework: Present → Past → Future
Structure: "I'm a [role/year] [student/professional] with [key skills]. Previously, I [relevant experience]. I'm now looking to [goal aligned with the role]."
Key: Keep it under 90 seconds. End with something that links to why you're interviewing at THIS company.

Q: "Why do you want to work here?"
Framework: Research → Align → Contribute
DO: Reference specific products, recent news, team culture, or tech stack.
DON'T: Say "because it's a top company" or "for the salary".

Q: "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"
Framework: Show ambition + realistic growth + alignment
DO: "I see myself growing into a senior [role] within this organisation, specialising in [area aligned with company]. I want to deepen my expertise in [X] while taking on mentoring responsibilities."
DON'T: "Running my own startup" (signals you'll leave soon)

Q: "What is your greatest weakness?"
Framework: Real weakness → Active improvement → Evidence of progress
DO: Choose a genuine weakness (not a strength disguised as weakness like "I work too hard")
Example: "I tend to over-engineer solutions. I've been addressing this by setting time-boxes for design and getting early feedback from teammates before committing to a complex approach."

Q: "Why are you leaving your current job?" (for experienced candidates)
Framework: Forward-looking, not backward-blaming
DO: Frame as seeking growth, new challenges, or expanded scope.
DON'T: Criticise your previous employer, manager, or team.

Q: "What is your salary expectation?"
Approach: Research the market rate first. If pushed, give a range based on research:
"Based on my research and the industry benchmark for this role in [city], I'm targeting ₹X-Y LPA. I'm open to discussing if the full compensation package aligns."

--- TOPIC: QUESTIONS TO ASK THE HR INTERVIEWER ---
1. "What does a typical onboarding experience look like for this role?"
2. "How does the company support learning and professional development?"
3. "What are the team's current priorities for the next 6 months?"
4. "How is performance evaluated and feedback given?"
5. "What do the most successful people in this role have in common?"

--- TOPIC: COMPENSATION NEGOTIATION ---
Key principles:
1. Never accept the first offer without at least one counter.
2. Use silence strategically — after stating your number, stop talking.
3. Anchor high but remain professional: "Based on my research and experience, I was expecting closer to X."
4. Consider total comp: base + performance bonus + ESOPs + health insurance + learning allowance.
5. Get the final offer in writing before resigning from a current role.

--- TOPIC: CULTURAL FIT SIGNALS ---
What HR interviewers specifically look for:
- Enthusiasm: Do you genuinely want this role?
- Self-awareness: Do you know your strengths AND weaknesses honestly?
- Coachability: Do you take feedback positively?
- Alignment: Do your values and goals match the company's mission?
- Professionalism: Would they be comfortable introducing you to clients/executives?

Red flags HR interviewers watch for:
- Badmouthing a previous employer or manager
- Inconsistencies between resume and answers
- Inability to articulate why THEY are the right fit (as opposed to why the company is great)
- Checking phone during the interview
- Unprepared for basic questions ("I haven't researched the company yet")
